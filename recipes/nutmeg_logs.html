Nutmeg Logs

These cookies are known in my family as "eggnog cookies" due to the fact that they taste exactly like eggnog. A glorious mix of nutmeg and rum flavouring will leave you thinking about Christmas everytime you eat these cookies.

These cookies are even a favorite of my father's - and considering he doesn't like sweets, that's high praise! While I typically prefer these cookies iced, you can make them without the icing if you want an slightly less sweet version of the treat.

Cookie Ingredients

  • 1 c. butter, softened
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tsp rum extract
  • 2-1/2 c. all-pupose flour
  • 1-1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • dash salt

Optional Frosting Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 3 cups confectioners' sugar
  • 1-1/2 to 2 tsp rum extract
  • 2 to 4 Tbs 2% milk
  • Ground nutmeg

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, cream butter and sugae until light and fluffy (5-7) minutes. Beat in egg and extract. Combine the flour, nutmeg, and salt; gradually add to the creamed mixture and mix well
  2. Divide dough into 3 portions. Roll each portion into 3/4 in think logs; chill until firm about 30 minutes. Cut into 2 in pieces. Place on an ungreased baking sheet; flatten slightly. Chill 15-20 minutes
  3. Bake at 350 degrees F until bottoms are lightly browned, 12-16 minutes. Cool for a few minutes before moving to wire racks to cool completely

Steps for Frosting

  1. In a large bowl, beat butter until fluffy. Beat in confectioners sugae, extract, and enough milk to achieve the desired consistency
  2. Frost cookies
  3. Press down with tines of a fork to create a bark like look
  4. Sprinkle with nutmeg
